About Me
In 2011, after completing a basic Java programming course in high school, I stumbled upon iOS development as a senior project. After a short amount of research, I cultivated a passion and was instantly fascinated by the vast ocean of design Apple provided for its developers. Welcome to iOS 4...

After many voluntary, countless nights up late learning the nooks and crannies of the iOS SDK, I was proudly able to launch my first application worldwide in the App Store prior to graduation. The product? Sand Sketch. The purpose? A graphical sketching application to send messages to friends which replicated finger drawing in the sand.

Nine years later, I continue to be the same ambitious and charismatic iOS developer. I gravitate towards new opportunities that allow me to bond with the product on a personal level. My philosophy is believing all applications should instill a joyful experience in the end user through functional and aesthetic layouts, trusted performance, simplistic navigation, and most importantly - a memorable solution!

For my career, I am currently employed by General Electric as a Software Engineer. My responsiblity includes designing, developing, and validating Java and Python-based applications for advanced control power plant turbomachinery.

iOS Portfolio
As I'm always eagerly looking to create new applications to meet the needs of family, friends, and clients, the projects below highlight a personal journey through some of my most unique projects, how they started, my passion towards them, and their end result.
Scout (2020)

Purpose

Scout is an up-and-coming native iOS application that serves as a location-based food and drink forum. It openly collects data from users and private venues/businesses to deliver live and local specials, deals, fun events to users. Coming soon!

Architecture

This project utilizes Swift 5.0 with the SwiftUI. User authentication is managed using Cloud Firestore (Google Firebase) and notifications are managed using OneSignal.

Inspiration

This app was purely inspired by the silent feeling followed by asking simple questions which should somehow have simple answers:

"What do we want to do today?"
"Where do we go for happy hour?"
"What fun events are happening around town?"

Seven days a week, this problem is common. It is the reason why asking your girlfriends or wife where they want to eat is always so difficult. It is the reason why you always end up going to your same spot, even though deep down you wish you could discover something new. Traditions are valuable, but new traditions are always meant to be made.

My vision for Scout is allowing a major user base to access free and accurate information for what is happening around them. Unlike Facebook and Yelp, Scout aims to dive into the nooks and crannies of what makes a business unique and special and highlight these qualities. During the Covid-19 outbreak, I found a more inspiring passion to accelerate development to help small businesses stay on their feet and bring the local communities together. Information is a powerful tool and nothing would make me happier than bringing joy to those who can discover something new, make a memory with friends and family, and maybe save a buck or two in the process.

Pace Chase (2019)

Purpose

Live worldwide in the App Store (for free), Pace Chase is a native iOS application which serves as a simple race pace and finish time calculator for runners of all levels. Available on iOS and watchOS!

Architecture

This project utilizes Swift 4.0 with UIKit and integrated seamlessly with watchOS. All user settings are managed using Core Data.

Available in the App Store

Inspiration

Two weeks leading up to a half marathon, I was searching the App Store for a minimal iOS + watchOS race pace application which provided flexible choices for viewing pacing per mile, but also hitting target times. When my search came up short, I took an impromptu shot at creating my own application. Three days later, it was complete, I was satisfied, and I even submitted it to the App Store for others to enjoy. Since submission, it was been downloaded over 1,200 times with over 60,000 user impressions.

Blue Ink (2017-2018)

Purpose

Blue Ink is a native iOS application which serves as a minimal dashboard for managing shopping and to-do lists. Whether creating, editing, or sharing lists, Blue Ink allows users to simulatenously work to complete lists across multiple users in realtime.

Architecture

This project utilized Swift 3.0 with UIKit. User authentication was managed using Realtime Database (Google Firebase) and notifications were managed using OneSignal.

Inspiration

We've all seen the Facebook groups about people claiming what they're going to bring to a party, yet we have to scroll to ensure duplicates aren't brought and something isn't forgotten. We've also been in the position to where you've been into a grocery store with friends or family and really wished you could "divide and conquer" to complete the shopping on a tight time schedule. This continual situation soon sparked my inspiration for Blue Ink. Sadly, the project was indefinitely delay to focus on my graduate school.
